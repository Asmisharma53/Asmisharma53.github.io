About

From Nepal to biomedical research in aging.

I have always been captivated by the intricate molecular mechanisms that govern cellular processes and how their dysregulation can lead to disease. As a PhD student at SIU School of Medicine, I study the modulation of the “pace of life,” a concept linking developmental rate to aging.

I am an international student from Nepal. I completed my Bachelor of Science in Medical Laboratory Technology at Pokhara University with Dean’s List honors and a merit-based scholarship. Through laboratory training, internships, field work, seminars, literature reviews, and student leadership, I developed strong interests in medical biochemistry, cancer biology, molecular biology, and translational research.

As president of the Pokhara University Biomedical Association of Students, I helped organize health camps, blood donation programs, public awareness campaigns, and scientific talks in collaboration with organizations such as Nepal Red Cross Society and Lions Clubs International. These experiences strengthened my leadership, teamwork, public speaking, and commitment to community service.

Research

Current and past research themes

Immune development and aging

Doctoral research on the impact of early-life dietary and genetic interventions on immune development and aging in the Geriatrics Division at SIU School of Medicine.

Macrophage biology

Poster work presented at the 6th Annual Midwest Aging Consortium on NRIP1 deletion and its role in macrophage polarization and inflammatory response.

Clinical biochemistry

Master’s research establishing reference intervals for routine liver function tests in adults from Gandaki Province, Nepal, supported by the University Grants Commission.

Community health research

Research experience in contraception knowledge, attitude, and practice studies, diabetes risk factors, lipid profiles, and multidrug-resistant Escherichia coli carriage.
